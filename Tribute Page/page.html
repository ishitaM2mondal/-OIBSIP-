Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He came from a humble family and rose to become one of India's greatest scientists and leaders.

He played a major role in India's missile and nuclear programs, earning the title “Missile Man of India.” His work at ISRO and DRDO made India stronger in science, technology, and defense.

In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11th President of India. He was loved by students and citizens because of his simplicity, humility, and inspiring personality.

Dr. Kalam always encouraged young people to dream big, work hard, and serve the nation. His life remains a symbol of dedication, knowledge, and patriotism.

Major Achievements

Missile Development

Led the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles for India's defense system.

ISRO Contribution

Contributed to India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

President of India

Served as the 11th President of India and became known as the “People’s President.”

Bharat Ratna

Received India's highest civilian award for his contribution to science and society.
